What this does, step by step
  1. Reads every row and parses Start Date (format MM/DD/YY HH:MMam).
  2. Keeps only rows inside the semester window you set.
  3. Drops any activity whose name contains an excluded phrase, plus any blank activity.
  4. Drops excluded statuses, keeping verified / attended records.
  5. Cleans the hours column to a number (e.g. "2 hrs" → 2).
  6. Roster: groups by student × activity and sums hours, recording first and last service dates. Output columns and order match the AdvanceID sheet, with NA in the columns you fill in by hand (Race/Ethnicity, First Gen, 402 Returner, Host Partner). Major comes from the enrollment-program field; Year in school from Graduation Date.
  7. OPS merge: each OPS Reflection row is folded into that student's OPS service row for the same semester — the semester is read from the service dates (Aug–Dec = Fall, Jan–May = Spring), not the activity name, so it keeps working no matter how a future term is named. Across a full year, Fall reflections land on Fall rows and Spring on Spring. If a reflection has no same-semester service row it folds into the student's closest OPS row instead, and if they have no OPS service row at all it's left in place — both cases are flagged in the review panel rather than dropped.
  8. Summary: per activity, totals hours, visits, distinct weeks, and distinct volunteers (reflections shown as their own activities here).

Column names are matched whether the export uses spaces (Event Name) or the R-style dots (Event.Name). Output files have no stray index column.

Runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, and no data is stored.
